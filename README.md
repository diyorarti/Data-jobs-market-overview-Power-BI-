# 📊 Data Jobs Market Overview Dashboard

## Overview
In this project, I analyze the data job market to uncover hidden patterns that are often not visible in individual job descriptions. The goal of this analysis is to gain insights into the data job market, including the availability of different roles, salary ranges, experience level requirements, and other important trends.

## 📈 Dataset 
The dataset used in this project contains information about jobs in the data industry, including job roles, salaries, experience levels, company characteristics, and work settings. It provides a comprehensive view of the data job market and allows analysis of trends in job demand and compensation.

## 📈 Dashboard Features

The Power BI dashboard provides an interactive analysis of the global data job market. It allows users to explore job demand, salary trends, work settings, and job categories through multiple visualizations and interactive elements.

### 1. Market Overview
The dashboard begins with key performance indicators that summarize the overall data job market, including:
- Total number of job postings
- Median yearly salary
- Median salary in local currency
- Salary currency used in the dataset
- Overall job rating indicator

These KPIs provide a quick snapshot of the market.

### 2. Job Demand by Role
A bar chart visualizes the number of job postings for different data-related roles such as:
- Data Engineer
- Data Scientist
- Data Analyst
- Machine Learning Engineer
- Applied Scientist

This helps identify the most in-demand positions in the data industry.

### 3. Salary Trends Over Time
A line chart shows the median salary trends from **2020 to 2023** across different experience levels:
- Entry-level
- Mid-level
- Senior
- Executive

This visualization highlights how compensation has evolved across years and experience levels.

### 4. Work Setting Distribution
A stacked bar chart illustrates how work settings are distributed across experience levels, comparing:
- Remote work
- Hybrid work
- In-person work

This helps analyze how workplace flexibility varies with seniority.

### 5. Job Category Analysis
A detailed table provides insights into different data job categories including:
- Machine Learning & AI
- Data Engineering
- Data Science & Research
- Data Analysis
- BI & Visualization
- Cloud & Database

The table displays job count, median salaries, and trend indicators for each category.

### 6. Job Title Drill-Through Analysis
Users can drill through to a dedicated page for deeper analysis of a specific job title.

The drill-through page includes:
- Median salary gauges
- Experience level distribution
- Company size distribution
- Job distribution by experience level
- Geographic job distribution by country

This feature enables a deeper exploration of specific roles in the data industry.

### 7. Interactive Filtering
The dashboard includes interactive filters such as:
- Job title slicer
- Cross-filtering between visuals

These allow users to dynamically explore the data and focus on specific roles or segments of the job market.


## 🔍 Key Insights

The dashboard analysis reveals several important insights about the global data job market.

### 1. Data Engineering and Data Science roles dominate the market
Data Engineer and Data Scientist positions show the highest number of job postings, indicating strong industry demand for professionals with advanced data processing and analytical skills.

### 2. Salaries increase significantly with experience
Senior and executive roles earn considerably higher salaries compared to entry-level and mid-level positions. This highlights the strong relationship between experience and compensation in the data industry.

### 3. Remote and hybrid work are widely adopted
The distribution of work settings shows that many data roles support remote or hybrid work environments, especially for senior-level positions.

### 4. Machine Learning and AI roles offer some of the highest salaries
Jobs within the **Machine Learning and AI category** tend to have higher median salaries compared to other data-related roles.

### 5. Data Engineering roles show strong demand
Data Engineering appears consistently across high job counts and competitive salaries, emphasizing its importance in modern data-driven organizations.

### 6. Senior-level positions dominate specialized roles
In some advanced positions such as **Machine Learning Infrastructure**, the majority of roles require senior-level experience, indicating that specialized roles often demand experienced professionals.

### 7. Medium and large companies drive hiring
Most job opportunities appear to come from medium and large organizations, suggesting that established companies invest more heavily in data teams.
